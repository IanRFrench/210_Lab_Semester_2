Carmel

Location

Carmel is located north of Indianapolis, Indiana, in the United States.

About

Carmel has a population of about 106,000 people and is ranked as one of the best places to live in the United States

History

Originally inhabited by the Delaware native Indians, Carmel transformed from a small Quaker settlement to a major suburban city.

Culture

Carmel is known for its heavy investment in the arts, including the Center For The Performing Arts and the Arts And Design District.

Attractions

Carmel has several attractions including the Monon Trail, and the smallest children's museum in the world.

Things To Do

  • Walk the Monon Trail for walking, running, or biking.
  • Explore Coxhall Gardens, a beautiful botanical garden with walking paths and sculptures.
  • Visit the Center For The Performing Arts for concerts, theater productions, and art exhibitions.
  • In the winter, stroll around Carmel Christkindlmarkt, a festive holiday market.
  • Take a walk around Clay Terrace, an outdoor shopping center with various shops and restaurants.
